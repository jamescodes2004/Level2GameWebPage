Evolution

by: James Ward

This game was made for my level 2 project at The League of Amazing Programmers.
Click here to download and play!

1. Why I Made This Game

I decided to make this game because initially I wanted to create an evolution simulator. Although my game didn't end up as expected, this was my attempt to allow the character to evolve to different stages.

2. What I Learned

I learned a lot during the process of making this game. One of the most time consuming things was making the different buttons to evolve the character to have different traits, so that is why I decided to make my game more simple. In testing I learned that the color of some of my backgrounds were too dark to see the text. This was a large issue that was extremely easy to fix.

3. Architecture of My Game

My game consists of many moving parts. The player is a squid trying to collect food to grow and upgradePoints to get faster. The enemies, which are fish, attempt to eat the food as well, but if they are bigger than the player they can eat them as well. However, if the player is bigger than them he/she can eat them. There is also a boat and fishing line that the player and the enemies have to avoid to remain alive.

4. Technical Implementation

My game involves array lists for enemies, food, and upgradePoints. I have a gameObject file that I where I keep most of the variables for those objects. I also used an objectManager to purge objects, create timers for when things spawn, and check collisions. For checking collisions in specific I used collision Boxes and a checkCollision method to check when a food was eaten or when an enemy hit the player. I also made the boat and hook move in random directions. The one function I'm most proud of is the algorithm I used to make it so that the enemies go to the closest food.

5. Most Challenging Part

The aspect of my game that I'm most proud of also happens to be the one that was the most challenging to implement. Initially, I accidentally made it so that the enemies would go to the closest y OR x, and not the food with the closest y and x combined. When I finally fixed that it seemed to work well, but then I must have done something to the code as now when I run the game the enemies usually go to the piece of food that was most recently created, unless its really far away.

6. Future Fixes and Updates

In the future, I would like to improve on the detection algorithm for the enemies, because as I said, sometimes it doesn't work. I would also like to make the text in the game look cleaner, and maybe mess around with some different fonts. Lastly, instead of automatically winning the game when you are big enough to eat the boat, I would like there to be an animation.
